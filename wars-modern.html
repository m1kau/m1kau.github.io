Browser Wars & Modern Era

Internet Explorer and Market Dominance

Microsoft released Internet Explorer in 1995 and bundled it with Windows. By the year 2000, Internet Explorer held more than 90% of the browser market. This move hurt Netscape's popularity and brought Microsoft into legal trouble. The U.S. government sued Microsoft for abusing its dominance, claiming it unfairly used Windows to force its browser on users.

Antitrust Case

The antitrust lawsuit showed how powerful browsers had become. Emails revealed that Microsoft actively tried to push Netscape out of the market. The court ruled that Microsoft acted unfairly, and the case became an early example of how tech companies can misuse control.

Firefox and Safari

As Internet Explorer slowed in development and became known for security issues, new browsers appeared. Mozilla launched Firefox in 2004 with features like tabbed browsing, pop-up blocking, and strong security. Apple released Safari in 2003 using its WebKit engine, making it the default browser for Macs and later iPhones.

Google Chrome

In 2008 Google released Chrome. It ran quickly, looked simple, and felt safe. Chrome included a V8 JavaScript engine and sandboxed tabs, so one crash would not affect the rest of the browser. Google open-sourced Chrome as Chromium, and it quickly became the most popular browser worldwide by the mid-2010s.

Microsoft Edge and the Chromium Era

Microsoft tried again with Edge in 2015, but switched to Chromium in 2019 after struggling with its original version. Other browsers like Opera also moved to Chromium. Now most browsers share Chromium, except Safari with WebKit and Firefox with Gecko. This raised concerns about one engine controlling too much of the web.
